# Zero Budget Marketing Engine - Client Acquisition Playbook

## Objective

Acquire qualified clients for Task Enterprise without paid ads by combining authority content, direct outreach, partnerships, community presence, lead magnets, and disciplined follow-up.

## Revenue Goal

Build a repeatable system that creates:

- awareness from high-trust content
- interest from clear problem/solution framing
- lead capture through useful offers
- qualification through forms, CRM tags, and outreach
- booked calls through consistent follow-up

## Ideal Client Profile

### Primary ICP

- SMB founder, operator, or team lead
- already experiencing workflow bottlenecks
- interested in AI, automation, or internal tooling
- has revenue or operational pressure now
- can make or strongly influence buying decisions

### Buying Signals

- mentions wasted time, bottlenecks, handoff issues, or repetitive tasks
- actively posting about growth, scaling, operations, or AI adoption
- hiring for ops, automation, implementation, or revops roles
- using spreadsheets, disconnected tools, or manual follow-up systems
- asking for faster execution, better visibility, or system cleanup

### Disqualifiers

- no urgent business pain
- hobby-stage operators with no budget or authority
- only wants generic “AI ideas”
- unclear business model
- no path to implementation

## Offer Ladder

### Top-of-Funnel Offers

- free checklist
- short audit
- tactical guide
- workflow teardown
- ROI calculator or lightweight template

### Mid-Funnel Offers

- strategy call
- tailored workflow review
- current-state automation audit
- quick wins report

### Bottom-of-Funnel Offers

- paid implementation sprint
- AI workflow buildout
- internal tool setup
- multi-agent operations system

## Acquisition Channels

### 1. Authority Content

Purpose:
- create trust
- explain problems clearly
- attract search and social traffic

Formats:
- LinkedIn posts
- X threads
- short videos
- blog posts
- case-study style breakdowns
- carousel or checklist posts

Content themes:
- automation bottlenecks
- AI workflow mistakes
- multi-agent operations
- internal dashboards and command centers
- lead handling and sales follow-up systems
- examples of manual work that should be automated

CTA path:
- content -> checklist or audit -> landing page -> CRM capture -> welcome sequence -> strategy call

### 2. Direct Outreach

Purpose:
- create immediate opportunities from high-fit prospects

Prospect sources:
- LinkedIn search
- founder communities
- Slack groups
- Discord communities
- newsletter creators
- warm network and referrals

Message structure:
1. show relevance
2. point to a business bottleneck
3. offer a useful insight or teardown
4. invite a short call or send a useful resource

Outreach rules:
- no spam blasts
- personalize first line
- connect message to visible business context
- give value before pitching
- track every touch in CRM

### 3. Partnerships

Target partners:
- agencies without automation depth
- consultants with operator audiences
- RevOps and ops advisors
- web designers serving SMB clients
- adjacent AI builders

Partnership motions:
- cross-promotion
- guest appearances
- referral swaps
- audit exchange
- bundled service offers

### 4. Community Engagement

Target spaces:
- Reddit communities where allowed
- founder/operator Slack groups
- LinkedIn comment networks
- niche Discord servers
- industry communities around ops, SaaS, and AI

Approach:
- answer questions
- post useful frameworks
- share small wins
- publish teardown-style responses
- route interested people to lead magnet or audit

### 5. SEO and Search Capture

Content targets:
- high-intent keywords tied to pain
- low-competition long-tail operational search terms
- service pages tied to automation outcomes

Priority article types:
- “how to automate X”
- “best workflow for Y”
- “AI system for Z role”
- “manual process replacement guide”

## Funnel Architecture

### Stage 1: Attention

Inputs:
- social posts
- SEO articles
- community comments
- outreach messages
- partner mentions

Success metric:
- clicks to owned surfaces

### Stage 2: Capture

Owned surfaces:
- landing page
- lead magnet page
- strategy call page
- resource page

Required fields:
- name
- email
- company
- team size
- lead goal
- source tags

Success metric:
- conversion rate by CTA and source

### Stage 3: Qualification

Qualification logic:
- fit with ICP
- urgency of pain
- implementation readiness
- likely budget or buying authority

Routing:
- high-fit -> call invite
- medium-fit -> nurture + value email
- low-fit -> newsletter and resource path

### Stage 4: Nurture

Nurture assets:
- welcome email sequence
- case studies
- tactical follow-ups
- problem-specific teardown

Success metric:
- reply rate
- click-through rate
- booked calls

### Stage 5: Conversion

Conversion mechanisms:
- strategy call
- diagnostic audit
- sprint proposal
- implementation plan

Success metric:
- calls booked
- proposals sent
- close rate

## Engagement Sequences

### Sequence A: Lead Magnet Opt-In

Day 0:
- deliver asset
- set expectation
- ask one diagnostic question

Day 1:
- send short lesson tied to pain

Day 3:
- send case study or workflow example

Day 5:
- invite to short strategy call

Day 7:
- final follow-up with direct value statement

### Sequence B: Cold Outreach

Touch 1:
- personalized opener + observed bottleneck

Touch 2:
- short practical suggestion

Touch 3:
- share relevant framework, teardown, or resource

Touch 4:
- direct call invite or “should I close this out?”

### Sequence C: Community to Call

Step 1:
- useful answer in public

Step 2:
- direct resource offer

Step 3:
- DM with specific next step

Step 4:
- call or audit invitation

## Weekly Execution Cadence

### Monday

- review prior week metrics
- choose one primary channel and one support channel
- lock 5-7 content assets
- assign outreach target list

### Tuesday

- publish long-form asset
- repurpose into short-form posts
- begin first outreach block

### Wednesday

- community engagement block
- partner outreach block
- update CRM and identify hot leads

### Thursday

- publish social proof, case study, or teardown
- run follow-up sequence
- review landing page and CTA performance

### Friday

- book calls
- review replies, conversions, and blockers
- decide what to scale, cut, or improve next week
