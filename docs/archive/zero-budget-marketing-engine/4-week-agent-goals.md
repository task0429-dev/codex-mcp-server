# Zero Budget Marketing Engine - 4 Week Agent Goals

## Purpose

Define explicit weekly goals for every Task Enterprise agent involved in the Zero Budget Marketing Engine so execution is measurable, owned, and reviewable.

## Week 1 - Audit, Positioning, and Tracking

### Abdi

- Define the primary ICP and two secondary ICP variants.
- Lock the core offer statement and business outcome promise.
- Approve the week 1 priority stack and decision rules.

### Atlas

- Audit all existing marketing channels, profiles, and published assets.
- Identify five content pillars aligned to ICP pain.
- Draft the first week content direction and CTA strategy.

### Ayub

- Define and document UTM structure and campaign naming rules.
- Set up tracking requirements for CTA clicks, form submissions, and lead source attribution.
- Prepare CRM tags and automation handoff requirements.

### Ahmed

- Create the working inventory of assets, channels, and reusable proof points.
- Build a keyword and topic research starter list.
- Organize documentation so the project has clean operating structure.

### Sygma

- Define the weekly execution cadence and reporting rhythm.
- Create the checklist for daily review, blockers, and handoff.
- Lock the weekly review format for TASK.

### Dame

- Support asset collection and local operational setup.
- Prepare any needed local folders, exports, or assembly tasks for content and lead magnet creation.
- Confirm tooling readiness for week 2 execution assets.

### Rex

- Review landing-path reliability and tracking blind spots.
- Identify technical funnel risks that could reduce conversions or attribution accuracy.
- Recommend instrumentation checks for forms, events, and response paths.

## Week 2 - Content Engine and Lead Capture

### Abdi

- Refine offer and messaging based on audit findings.
- Approve the first lead magnet topic and funnel promise.
- Review early traction indicators and remove weak channels.

### Atlas

- Produce the first week of authority-content messaging and distribution plan.
- Draft five outreach angles tied to visible business pain.
- Build the content-to-CTA path across social, community, and site surfaces.

### Ayub

- Implement or prepare landing-page and lead-capture plumbing.
- Wire CRM routing and tag logic for source and CTA tracking.
- Set up welcome sequence or automation flow requirements.

### Ahmed

- Produce topic outlines for the first five content pieces.
- Expand the keyword map into intent-based content targets.
- Draft the structure for the first case-study or teardown content asset.

### Sygma

- Create the week 2 operating board with content, outreach, and follow-up checkpoints.
- Enforce execution cadence for publishing and follow-up.
- Track blockers and missed handoffs before they compound.

### Dame

- Assemble lead magnet assets, supporting files, or local production materials.
- Help prepare webinar, PDF, checklist, or template packaging if needed.
- Support publishing logistics for content deployment.

### Rex

- Validate landing-page and form behavior before traffic is pushed.
- Test conversion-path technical integrity.
- Flag friction points in page speed, form handling, or CTA flow.

## Week 3 - Outreach, Partnerships, and Qualification

### Abdi

- Review pipeline quality and redefine qualification rules if needed.
- Approve the partner and community engagement priority list.
- Guide focus toward the highest-signal channel and outreach motion.

### Atlas

- Run direct outreach messaging and partnership copy strategy.
- Support community engagement scripts and response patterns.
- Refine CTA language based on reply and click behavior.

### Ayub

- Maintain outreach tracking and CRM stage movement.
- Support automation for follow-up, nurture, or response tagging.
- Keep reporting surfaces current for lead quality and response rate.

### Ahmed

- Build and refine prospect lists, community targets, and keyword-backed opportunities.
- Draft follow-up assets, teardown notes, and supporting copy.
- Document learnings from replies and lead behavior.

### Sygma

- Run the weekly accountability review across all active motions.
- Ensure every lead source has a next action and owner.
- Maintain clean status visibility for TASK.

### Dame

- Support operational execution for live outreach blocks, partner packages, and local follow-up assets.
- Assist with webinar or event setup if live sessions are introduced.
- Help keep the execution queue moving without local bottlenecks.

### Rex

- Audit drop-off points between lead capture, nurture, and booked-call stages.
- Check automation reliability and handoff integrity between systems.
- Recommend fixes for response delays or routing issues.

## Week 4 - Conversion, Optimization, and Scale Decision

### Abdi

- Review full-month performance and identify what should scale.
- Decide which acquisition motions remain core, secondary, or retired.
- Approve the next 30-day operating direction.

### Atlas

- Double down on top-performing messaging and channels.
- Create optimized versions of best-performing posts, CTAs, and outreach hooks.
- Define the next content and acquisition growth loop.

### Ayub

- Tighten conversion flow and implement priority automation fixes.
- Improve reporting fidelity for lead-to-call and call-to-close visibility.
- Prepare the system for repeatable month-two execution.

### Ahmed

- Consolidate research, keyword wins, lead themes, and proven content angles.
- Produce a final insights pack for what resonated with prospects.
- Clean and structure the project docs for month-two reuse.

### Sygma

- Deliver the final execution review with wins, misses, blockers, and process improvements.
- Lock the repeatable weekly cadence for the next cycle.
- Ensure all open actions have owners and deadlines.

### Dame

- Finalize operational assets, exports, and delivery-ready supporting materials.
- Assist with handoff packaging for next-cycle execution.
- Help close execution gaps that block rollout of the winning motions.

### Rex

- Deliver a final conversion and systems audit.
- Identify technical weaknesses before scaling traffic and outreach volume.
- Recommend hardening steps for month-two reliability.

## Weekly Review Questions

Use these questions at the end of every week:

1. What did each agent complete versus what was assigned?
2. Which channel created the best qualified attention?
3. Where did leads stall or drop off?
4. Which assets or messages produced the strongest response?
5. What should be doubled down on next week?
6. What should be cut, simplified, or fixed immediately?
